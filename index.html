FEATURED PLACES TO VISIT

KUALA LUMPUR


The capital city of Malaysia and home to the tallest twin towers in the world. Its meaning in English is "muddy estuary". It is the commercial, cultural and transportation hub of Malaysia.

Link to page

PENANG


Listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is recognised as Malaysia's Food Capital. Popular for its outstanding street art and rich history as a British commercial port. It is a city with a wide range of cultural influences.

Link to page

LANGKAWI


An island in the state of Kedah, just west off the coastline. It is the oldest land in Malaysia, dating from 550 million years ago. It is a designated UNESCO World Geopark since 2007.

Link to page

MELAKA/MALACCA


Listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, along with Penang, since 2008. Founded in the late 14th century, Melaka would be colonised by the Portuguese in 1511, then the Dutch in 1641, and eventually the British in 1824. It was one of the Strait Settlements that were directly ruled by the British.

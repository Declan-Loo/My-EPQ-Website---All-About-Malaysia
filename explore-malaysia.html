MAJOR CITIES

KUALA LUMPUR

The capital city of Malaysia and home to the tallest twin towers in the world. Its meaning in English is "muddy estuary". It is the commercial, cultural and transportation hub of Malaysia.

PENANG

Listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is recognised as Malaysia's Food Capital. Popular for its outstanding street art and rich history as a British commercial port. It is a city with a wide range of cultural influences.


LANGKAWI

An island in the state of Kedah, just west off the coastline. It is the oldest land in Malaysia, dating from 550 million years ago. It is a designated UNESCO World Geopark since 2007.

MELAKA

Listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, along with Penang, since 2008. Founded in the late 14th century, Melaka would be colonised by the Portuguese in 1511, then the Dutch in 1641, and eventually the British in 1824. It was one of the Strait Settlements that were directly ruled by the British.


POPULAR TOURIST ATTRACTIONS

GUNUNG MULU NATIONAL PARK

A UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Sarawak. Gunung Mulu National Park has limestone karst and massive cave formations. The site is surrounded by three mountains: Gunung Api, Gunung Benerat, and Gunung Buda, which are limestone mountains. Meanwhile, Gunung Mulu, which the site is named after, is a sandstone mountain.

The limestone was formed by the pressure of two crushing land plates for 20 million years and the deposition of rain and groundwater, respectively. The rainwater and groundwater caused limestone erosion, resulting in cave systems like chambers, underground rivers, and speleothems, only found in tropical areas. Gunung Mulu is also home to a variety of flora and fauna.

SIPADAN ISLAND

Regarded as one of the top places to go diving in the world. There are 12 different dive spots around the island. It is the only oceanic island in Malaysia. Sipadan is known for having a lot of green and hawksbill turtles, which mate on the island. There are also at least three thousand different fish species and hundreds of coral species found in the area. The island is also covered by jungle, and home to tropical birds.


MOUNT KINABALU

Mount Kinabalu is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, located in Sabah. With an altitude at 4,095m (13,465 ft) above sea level, it is Malaysiaâ€™s highest peak. The mountain can be scaled via an 8.8km trek, taking two days and one night to complete, both up and down the mountain, with a midnight stay at the mountain huts. The mountain is scalable for anybody, fit and healthy. Kinabalu Park is the site that surrounds the mountain.

With over five thousand different plant species, originating from the Himalayan, Australasian, and Indo-Malaysian regions, it is regarded as one of the most significant biodiversity sites in the world. There is a variety of activities you can do at Kinabalu Park including trekking, hiking, camping, watching wildlife, plant spotting and photography!

ABOUT KUALA LUMPUR

Malaysia's capital city is Kuala Lumpur. It is the commercial, cultural and transportation hub in Malaysia. Its name means "muddy estuary" in English. In 1857, a group of Chinese tin miners started the city of Kuala Lumpur at the confluence of the Klang and Gombak rivers. Over time, Kuala Lumpur developed as a tin mining location, even when the jungle was malaria-infested at that time. In 1880, it became the state capital. In 1895, it was picked as the capital of the Federated Malay States.

After Malaysia got its independence, Kuala Lumpur urbanised swiftly, and in the early 21st century, the administrative capital was moved from Kuala Lumpur to Putrajaya, which is just south of Kuala Lumpur. Kuala Lumpur continued to be Malaysiaâ€™s capital city.

PLACES TO GO

Petronas Twin Towers

Petronas Twin Towers is the world's tallest twin tower at 452m (1,483ft). Each tower has 88 floors. Once the world's tallest tower upon its completion, it surpassed the Sears Tower in 1992. The Petronas Twin Towers are based on Islamic patterns designed by Argentinian architect Cesar Pelli. A sky bridge on floors 41 and 42 connects the two towers, and there is an observation deck near the top of the tower.

Batu Caves

A Hindu temple built inside a cave. The temple honours the Hindu god, Lord Murugan. It is the place where the Thaipusam festival is held every year. To get to the temple, you have to climb 272 steps. Every day, people who worship there come to make offerings. Because Hindus care a lot about animals, they are free to roam around the temple. At the bottom of the stairs is the gold-covered statue of Lord Murugan, which is the tallest in the world.
